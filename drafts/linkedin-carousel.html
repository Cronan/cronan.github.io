Semantic ablation

Not hallucination, where the model invents what is not there.

The opposite.

The model destroying what is there. Your phrasing, your rhythm, your willingness to leave something unresolved. Smoothed into the same three-paragraph shape with the same measured confidence about everything.

The wrong fight

Detectors try to catch AI text.
Humanisers try to evade those detectors.

This is an arms race about the signal, not the substance.

A humaniser that swaps "delve" for "explore" to fool a classifier has not fixed the writing. It has laundered it.

Structural patterns

The patterns that do the most damage are about shape, not vocabulary.

  • Sentence length uniformity - everything clusters around 15-25 words. Metronomic.
  • Paragraph length uniformity - every paragraph the same height. Three to five sentences, tiling like bricks.
  • Epistemic uniformity - flat certainty on every claim. No hedging, no doubt, no being wrong.
  • Register lock - one tone from start to finish. No drift, no slang, no tightening when the stakes rise.
5 / 10

The vocabulary tells

25x increase in "delves" in academic papers, 2022 to 2024.

  • "Serves as," "functions as," "stands as" where "is" would do
  • Everything is pivotal, crucial, transformative. If everything is significant, nothing is.
  • Clean because/therefore chains where reality is messy. Humans say "and then" when the cause is unclear.
6 / 10

What the model removes

Strip the patterns and you are still only halfway.

  • Emotional reaction. It describes a production outage in the same tone it uses for a product launch.
  • Opinion. Every side presented with equal weight. Human writers lean somewhere. Even cautious ones.
  • Specific detail. "A team I worked with" versus "the four of us at the tail end of that November sprint."
  • Time. Everything in vague present tense. No anchoring, no "I remember the week before launch."
7 / 10

Editing, not evasion

The alternative to both detection and evasion is editing. Not "make this text pass a classifier" but "make this text better."

Strip structural tells first. Then vocabulary. Then add back what the model removed: opinion, reaction, specific detail, temporal anchoring.

A piece with zero rough edges is itself suspicious.
